Worked example — a 5 mg AOD-9604 vial

Take a 5 mg lyophilized AOD-9604 vial reconstituted with 2 mL of bacteriostatic water. Concentration = 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5 mg/mL (2500 mcg/mL). A 300 mcg research aliquot is 300 ÷ 2500 = 0.12 mL, which reads as about 12 units on a U-100 insulin syringe. That vial yields roughly 16.67 aliquots of that size. How the reconstitution math works

The total AOD-9604 in a vial is fixed at manufacture. The bacteriostatic water you add only sets the concentration. Concentration (mg/mL) = vial milligrams ÷ millilitres of water; multiply by 1,000 for mcg/mL. Divide your per-aliquot microgram target by that mcg/mL figure to get the draw volume in millilitres, then multiply by 100 to read it as units on a U-100 insulin syringe (which shows 100 units per millilitre). The same arithmetic works for every compound — only the numbers change. The general-purpose peptide reconstitution calculator covers any vial size.

Choosing your water volume

More dilute (more water) gives a larger, easier-to-measure draw and less rounding error on small aliquots; more concentrated (less water) means fewer total millilitres to store and a smaller draw. Most researchers pick a volume that lands their typical AOD-9604 aliquot in the readable middle of a U-100 syringe. The 2 mL shown above is a common starting point for a 5 mg vial, not a requirement.

Storage and purity verification

Keep lyophilized AOD-9604 frozen or refrigerated until use, and refrigerate the reconstituted vial protected from light. Before trusting any concentration figure, confirm the vial's stated mass against its lot documentation — read the certificate of analysis guide and the peptide storage guide. Frequently asked questions

How much bacteriostatic water should I add to a 5 mg AOD-9604 vial?

There is no single correct volume — the water only sets the concentration, not the amount of AOD-9604 in the vial. A common research choice for a 5 mg vial is 2 mL of bacteriostatic water, which gives 2.5 mg/mL (2500 mcg/mL). Adding less water concentrates the solution; adding more dilutes it and makes small aliquots easier to measure. Use the calculator above to model any volume.

What concentration does a 5 mg AOD-9604 vial reconstitute to?

Concentration (mg/mL) equals vial milligrams divided by millilitres of water. A 5 mg vial in 2 mL of bacteriostatic water is 2.5 mg/mL, or 2500 mcg/mL. The calculator converts both automatically.

How do I convert a microgram target into insulin-syringe units for AOD-9604?

Divide the per-aliquot microgram figure by the concentration in mcg/mL to get the volume in mL, then multiply by 100 for U-100 syringe units. At 2500 mcg/mL, a 300 mcg aliquot is 0.12 mL — about 12 units on a U-100 insulin syringe.

How should reconstituted AOD-9604 be stored?

Lyophilized AOD-9604 is generally kept frozen or refrigerated before reconstitution. Once mixed with bacteriostatic water (which contains ~0.9% benzyl alcohol to inhibit bacterial growth) a research vial is typically refrigerated and protected from light. Follow your lot's release documentation and standard cold-chain handling for your facility.
