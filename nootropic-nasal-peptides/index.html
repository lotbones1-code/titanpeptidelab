Questions researchers ask

Before you order.

What are nootropic nasal peptides?
They are neuropeptides studied for cognition, mood, sleep, or bonding research that are delivered intranasally — the route most of their research literature uses. Titan's line includes Semax, Selank, DSIP, and oxytocin as pre-formatted 15mL intranasal sprays. All are supplied strictly for in-vitro laboratory research, not for human use.
Why are these peptides sold as nasal sprays?
The published research for compounds like Semax, Selank, and oxytocin is largely built around intranasal administration. A pre-formatted spray removes the reconstitution and draw-up steps a lyophilized vial requires and maps directly onto how the compounds are studied. The compound itself is the same as the vial form where one exists.
Which nootropic nasal peptide should I start with?
It depends on the research angle: Semax for cognition/attention and BDNF signalling, Selank for anxiolytic/regulatory work, DSIP for sleep and circadian research, oxytocin for social-bonding and stress research. Semax and Selank are frequently studied together and available as a $105 stack. Titan makes no human-use or efficacy claims.
Do the nasal sprays come with a COA?
Yes — each spray ships with a lot-matched release sheet on request, keyed to the lot code on the bottle, with an HPLC main-peak purity result and a mass-spec identity confirmation, rather than a stock spec sheet reused across batches.
Are nootropic nasal peptides approved for human use?
No. Research-grade Semax, Selank, DSIP, and oxytocin are not approved for human use. Titan supplies them strictly as research-use-only compounds for in-vitro laboratory work — not for human or animal consumption, and not for diagnostic, therapeutic, or preventative use.
