DSIP vs Selank: same nasal format, two unrelated peptides.

Researchers line up DSIP and Selank because both arrive as metered nasal sprays studied in neuroscience and behavioural models — and because the catalog puts them next to each other. But they are not two takes on one idea. DSIP (delta-sleep-inducing peptide) is a short endogenous nonapeptide first isolated from work on sleep regulation. Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide engineered from the immunopeptide tuftsin and studied in anxiety- and cognition-focused research. Different sequences, different origins, different research questions — the comparison exists so a researcher picks by the pathway under study, not by which is 'stronger.' This page lays out the split, the nasal form both share, and the release-sheet standard. Research framing only: no dosing, no human-use, no behavioural-outcome claims.

Different sequences, different origins

This is the load-bearing distinction. DSIP is a nine-amino-acid endogenous peptide (Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu) identified in early sleep-physiology research. Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide built on tuftsin, an immunomodulatory fragment, and was designed for anxiety- and memory-related research models. They do not belong to the same family and were not derived from the same biology, so treating them as interchangeable is the mistake this comparison fixes.

Questions researchers ask

Before you order.

Are DSIP and Selank the same kind of peptide?
No. DSIP is a nine-residue endogenous peptide associated with sleep-regulation research. Selank is a synthetic seven-residue analog of tuftsin studied in anxiety- and cognition-focused research. They are unrelated sequences from different origins, which is exactly why researchers compare them rather than treat them as one product. This is research framing, not a human-use comparison.
Why would a researcher choose DSIP over Selank, or the other way around?
The choice follows the research question. Work centred on sleep-regulation models points toward DSIP; work centred on tuftsin-derived anxiolytic or nootropic pathways points toward Selank. Because the two were derived for different purposes, one does not stand in for the other — the comparison is about matching the compound to the question, with no efficacy or outcome claim implied.
Do DSIP and Selank need to be reconstituted?
Not at Titan — both ship as metered nasal sprays rather than lyophilized vials, so there is no reconstitution step or bacteriostatic-water dilution. That is the one practical thing the two share. It is a handling convenience only and does not make the molecules comparable; storage and lot-matched documentation still apply to each.
What should a DSIP or Selank COA show?
Compound-specific identity by mass confirming the correct sequence — the DSIP nonapeptide or the Selank heptapeptide — plus a purity percentage resolved by HPLC, with the lot number on the certificate matching the lot code on the unit you receive. A marketing badge that says 'tested' with no lot match and no mass identity is not verification.
Are DSIP or Selank for human use?
No. Both are supplied by Titan strictly for in-vitro laboratory research. Neither is for human or animal consumption, diagnostic, therapeutic, or preventative use, and no sleep, anxiety, mood, or cognition outcome is claimed for either. Nothing on this page is a dosing schedule or a human-use protocol.
