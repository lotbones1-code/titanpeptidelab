Ipamorelin vs sermorelin · research comparison

Ipamorelin vs sermorelin: two different ways to study GH signalling.

Ipamorelin and sermorelin both appear in growth-hormone research, but they act on different receptors and are not interchangeable. Ipamorelin is a selective ghrelin-receptor (GHS-R) secretagogue; sermorelin is a GHRH analogue acting on the GHRH receptor. This page compares the two as research compounds and explains why ipamorelin is most often studied alongside CJC-1295 rather than sermorelin.

Two different receptors

Ipamorelin is a selective growth-hormone secretagogue acting on the ghrelin/GHS receptor — it mimics ghrelin's signalling without the appetite or cortisol/prolactin effects of older GHRPs. Sermorelin is a GHRH (1-29) analogue acting on the GHRH receptor — it mimics the upstream hypothalamic releasing hormone. They engage the GH axis from two different entry points.

Why ipamorelin is paired with CJC-1295

In the research literature ipamorelin (a GHS-R secretagogue) is most often paired with CJC-1295 (a GHRH analogue) rather than with sermorelin — because pairing a ghrelin-receptor agonist with a GHRH-pathway agonist engages two complementary mechanisms at once. Sermorelin and CJC-1295 are both GHRH-pathway compounds, so they overlap rather than complement.

Stability and format

Sermorelin (GHRH 1-29) is comparatively short-lived in solution. CJC-1295 was engineered as a longer-acting GHRH analogue, which is why it — not sermorelin — is the GHRH-side partner in most modern research blends. Before you order.

What is the difference between ipamorelin and sermorelin?
They act on different receptors. Ipamorelin is a selective ghrelin/GHS-receptor secretagogue (GHRP class) that mimics ghrelin signalling; sermorelin is a GHRH (1-29) analogue acting on the GHRH receptor. They engage the growth-hormone axis from two different entry points and are not interchangeable in research design.
Why is ipamorelin paired with CJC-1295 and not sermorelin?
Pairing a ghrelin-receptor secretagogue (ipamorelin) with a GHRH-pathway agonist engages two complementary mechanisms. CJC-1295 is the GHRH-side partner because it is an engineered, longer-acting GHRH analogue, whereas sermorelin (GHRH 1-29) is short-lived in solution. Pairing ipamorelin with sermorelin would not add the same complementary stability.

Is CJC-1295 better than sermorelin?
They are not directly competing — both are GHRH-pathway compounds, but CJC-1295 was engineered for a longer duration of action than sermorelin (GHRH 1-29), which is why it appears in most modern research blends. The cjc-1295-vs-ipamorelin page covers the GHRH-vs-GHS distinction in detail.
Are these compounds approved for human use?
No. Research-grade ipamorelin, CJC-1295, and sermorelin sold as reagents are not approved for human use. Titan supplies its blend strictly as a research-use-only compound for in-vitro laboratory work — not for human or animal consumption, and not for diagnostic, therapeutic, or preventative use.
