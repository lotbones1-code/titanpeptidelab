Reading the certificate

Five gaps that mean the document hasn't proven what it claims.

DAC vs no-DAC is an identity question, not a branding one

CJC-1295 with DAC and CJC-1295 without DAC (Mod-GRF 1-29) are distinct molecules with distinct masses. A certificate that resolves the question states which one it characterized and reports a mass-spec weight to match. If the document is vague on DAC status, the analytical work behind it is incomplete.

A blend needs the ratio shown, not just asserted

The selling point of a CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin blend is the ratio. That means the COA should let you see two components and a balance close to the labeled split. One merged purity figure hides exactly the variable a buyer is paying to verify. Treat per-component data as the standard, not a bonus.

Mass spec answers what HPLC cannot

HPLC purity and mass-spec identity do different jobs. Purity tells you how clean the main peak is; mass tells you what the peak actually is. For a two-peptide blend with a DAC ambiguity on one component, the measured molecular weights are the part of the certificate that settles identity.
