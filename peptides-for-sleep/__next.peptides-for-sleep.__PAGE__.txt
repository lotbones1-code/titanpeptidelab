It is a naturally occurring nonapeptide (sequence Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu) first isolated in 1977 by Monnier and Schoenenberger from the cerebral venous blood of sleeping rabbits, and named for its association with delta-wave (deep, slow-wave) sleep. So the accurate framing of 'peptides for sleep' is 'compounds studied in a sleep context,' not 'proven sleep drugs.'
