Selank vs Oxytocin · research comparison

Selank vs oxytocin: same nasal format, two unrelated peptide families.

Selank and oxytocin get searched together because both ship as metered nasal sprays studied around mood, stress, and social-behaviour neuroscience — but the resemblance ends at the bottle. Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide derived from tuftsin, an endogenous immunomodulatory fragment; oxytocin is a cyclic nine-residue neurohypophyseal peptide that signals through the oxytocin receptor. They come from different biological origins, carry different structures, and act on different systems, so a researcher chooses between them by which pathway is under study — not because one is a stronger version of the other. Different origins, different systems

This is the load-bearing distinction. Selank is a synthetic analog of tuftsin — a naturally occurring immunomodulatory tetrapeptide — extended to a stabilised heptapeptide for research. Oxytocin is a neurohypophyseal (posterior-pituitary) hormone acting at the oxytocin receptor. One descends from an immune-fragment lineage studied in anxiolytic and neuro-immune models; the other is a classic social-behaviour neuropeptide. They are not variants of one idea, so treating them as interchangeable is the mistake the comparison corrects.

What Selank is, in research

Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro) built around the tuftsin sequence, studied in anxiolytic, immunomodulatory, and nootropic research models. Its proline-rich tail is what gives it stability over native tuftsin. Titan supplies it as a metered nasal spray for laboratory work — described strictly in sequence and research terms, with no human-use, behavioural-outcome, or therapeutic claim attached. Researchers reach for it when the question sits in the tuftsin / neuro-immune neighbourhood rather than the oxytocin-receptor one.

What oxytocin is, in research

Oxytocin is a cyclic nine-amino-acid peptide (a nonapeptide) closed by a single disulfide bridge, endogenous to mammals and long studied in social-, bonding-, and affiliative-behaviour neuroscience. In the literature it is a tool for probing oxytocin-receptor signalling, not a performance compound. Titan stocks it as a nasal spray for research framed entirely in receptor and laboratory terms — the contrast with Selank is the whole point of comparing them.

What each release sheet must confirm

Identity confirmation is structure-specific. Selank is a linear proline-rich heptapeptide, so a release sheet should confirm the full seven-residue sequence by mass — a chain truncated before the proline tail is the classic synthesis defect — and read purity by HPLC, where the multiple prolines drive peak shape. Oxytocin is cyclic, so its sheet must confirm the disulfide bridge is correctly formed (the open-chain form has near-identical composition but is a different molecule) on top of mass identity and HPLC purity. Are Selank and oxytocin the same kind of peptide?
No. Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide derived from tuftsin, an immunomodulatory fragment, and is studied in anxiolytic and neuro-immune research. Oxytocin is a cyclic nine-residue neurohypophyseal peptide that signals through the oxytocin receptor. They have different origins, different structures, and act on different systems — which is exactly why researchers compare them rather than treat them as one product. This is research framing, not a human-use comparison.
Why would a researcher choose Selank over oxytocin, or the other way around?
The choice follows the pathway under study. Work in the tuftsin, neuro-immune, or anxiolytic-model neighbourhood points to Selank; work centred on the oxytocin receptor and social-behaviour signalling points to oxytocin. Because the systems do not overlap, one cannot stand in for the other — the comparison is about matching the compound to the research question, with no efficacy or outcome claim implied.
Selank and oxytocin are both nasal sprays — does that make them similar?
Only in handling. Both ship as metered nasal sprays rather than lyophilized vials at Titan, so neither needs reconstitution or bacteriostatic-water dilution. That shared delivery format is a practical convenience, not a sign of molecular similarity — the sequences, structures, and receptor systems are entirely different. Storage and lot-matched documentation still apply to each independently.
What should a Selank or oxytocin release sheet confirm?
For Selank, the sheet should confirm the full seven-residue sequence by mass (a chain truncated before its proline-rich tail is the common defect) with purity by HPLC. For oxytocin, it must additionally confirm the disulfide bridge is correctly formed, since the open-chain form shares the composition but is a different molecule. Are Selank or oxytocin for human use?
No. Both are supplied by Titan strictly for in-vitro laboratory research.
