PT-141 vs Melanotan-2 · melanocortin research comparison

PT-141 vs Melanotan-2: same pathway, different selectivity.

PT-141 and Melanotan-2 are family — both are synthetic melanocortin-receptor agonists, and PT-141 (bremelanotide) is in fact a metabolite of Melanotan-2. The difference researchers care about is receptor selectivity. Melanotan-2 (MT-II) is a broad, comparatively non-selective melanocortin agonist with notable MC1-receptor (pigmentation-pathway) activity. PT-141 is studied chiefly at the MC4 receptor and lacks the MC1-dominant pigmentation profile. This page compares the two as research compounds — mechanism and target, not human-use or dosing — and notes which one Titan actually stocks. Research use only.

How the two are related

Melanotan-2 is the parent compound; PT-141 (bremelanotide) is a metabolite of it. That shared lineage is exactly why they get cross-searched — they sit on the same melanocortin pathway. But they are not interchangeable: the metabolism that produces PT-141 strips much of Melanotan-2's broader receptor activity, leaving a more MC4-weighted profile. Same family tree, different branch.

The receptor-selectivity difference

Melanotan-2 is a comparatively non-selective melanocortin agonist that engages multiple melanocortin receptors, including MC1 — the receptor associated in the literature with the pigmentation pathway. PT-141 is studied predominantly at MC4 and does not carry the same MC1-dominant pigmentation profile. The selectivity gap is the single most useful distinction between them as research targets.
