CJC-1295 vs Ipamorelin: two receptors, one blend.

CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin are the two most cross-searched growth-hormone-secretagogue research peptides, so buyers compare them constantly — but they aren't rivals. CJC-1295 is a GHRH-receptor analog (supplied with or without the DAC modification, i.e. Modified GRF 1-29); Ipamorelin is a short, selective ghrelin-receptor pentapeptide. They act at two different receptors, which is exactly why research protocols pair them — and why Titan supplies them as a single blended vial. This page lays out what each is, the DAC-vs-no-DAC molecular-weight check on the CJC-1295 COA, why a blend certificate has to confirm both peptides, and what to verify before sourcing either.

Two receptors, not two rivals

The structural distinction researchers care about: CJC-1295 is a GHRH-receptor analog, while Ipamorelin is a selective ghrelin / GHS-receptor agonist. They are studied at two different receptors, which is precisely why research protocols pair them rather than choose between them — and why Titan supplies them as a single blended vial. The common “CJC-1295 vs Ipamorelin” question usually resolves to “the blend.” Titan handles both strictly as research compounds, never as substitutes for any approved medication.

On CJC-1295, read DAC vs no-DAC first

The single biggest sourcing trap in this pair is that “CJC-1295” is a trade name used for two different molecules: one with DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) and one without it (Modified GRF 1-29). Those carry different masses, so the molecular weight on the release sheet — not the product label — is what tells you which one you actually received. Confirm the reported mass against the form the supplier claims to ship before trusting anything else on the page.

Blend COA red flags→

A blend means the COA must prove both

Verifying a single-peptide vial is one mass check; a two-peptide blend is not. The release sheet has to confirm each component's identity by mass and resolve the purity of both — a single blended purity number quietly hides which half is short or under-represented. That paper-trail standard — a lot-matched in-house release sheet referenced to the lot code on the unit, against an HPLC purity target with identity confirmation — is what separates a documented supplier from a blind one, and it is the only claim Titan makes about these compounds.

How to verify a COA→

Choose by the receptor your model needs

Pick by what your research model is built around: GHRH-receptor work points to CJC-1295 — and there, confirm DAC vs no-DAC; ghrelin / GHS-receptor work points to Ipamorelin; the paired-secretagogue model points to the blend. Avoid any supplier that attaches growth, muscle, anti-aging, dosing, or human-use claims to either; that marketing language is a compliance red flag, not science.
